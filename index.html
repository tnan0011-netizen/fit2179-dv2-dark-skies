Where Did the Stars Go?

Mapping Australia's Darkest Skies

Australia still has some of the world's most spectacular night skies, but artificial light from cities makes the stars harder to see for many people. This visualisation explores where night-time light is strongest, where darkness remains, and what makes a good stargazing location.

Key idea

Bright city areas create sky glow, while remote inland areas usually offer darker skies.

2

Where is the sky brightest?

Coastal cities and highly populated regions tend to produce stronger night-time glow.

3

Where can Australians still see the stars?

Dark skies are not evenly distributed. This section highlights recognised dark-sky places and darker regions.

4

Who has access to dark skies?

Dark skies often exist far from where most people live, so access depends on population and remoteness.

5

What makes the best stargazing location?

Good stargazing depends on darkness, clear skies, and practical access.

Conclusion

While many Australians live under bright urban skies, large parts of the continent remain dark and full of stars. Protecting and understanding these places can help future generations continue to experience the night sky.
